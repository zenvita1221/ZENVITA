Stress

Stress is the body’s natural physical and mental response to pressure, challenges, or difficult situations. It occurs when a person feels overwhelmed or unable to cope with the demands placed on them. Stress can be caused by many factors such as work pressure, exams, financial problems, health issues, family conflicts, or major life changes. There are two main types of stress: acute stress, which is short-term and happens in response to immediate situations, and chronic stress, which lasts for a long time and can affect a person’s well-being. Common symptoms of stress include headaches, fatigue, irritability, anxiety, difficulty concentrating, and trouble sleeping. If stress continues for a long period, it can negatively affect both mental and physical health.
