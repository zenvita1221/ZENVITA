Hangover

A hangover is the group of unpleasant symptoms that can occur after drinking too much alcohol. It usually happens the next day and may include headache, nausea, dizziness, thirst, fatigue, and sensitivity to light or sound. Hangovers occur because alcohol causes dehydration, irritates the stomach, disrupts sleep, and leads to the buildup of toxic substances as the body breaks down alcohol. The best ways to prevent a hangover are drinking alcohol in moderation, staying hydrated, and eating before or while drinking. Recovery typically involves rest, drinking water or electrolyte fluids, and allowing the body time to process and eliminate the alcohol. 🍶😵‍💫
