skin disease

A skin disease is any condition that affects the skin, the body’s largest organ. Skin diseases can cause symptoms such as redness, itching, swelling, rashes, or changes in skin color and texture. They may be caused by infections (bacteria, viruses, or fungi), allergies, genetic factors, poor hygiene, or environmental conditions. Common examples include Acne, Eczema, Psoriasis, and Ringworm. While some skin diseases are mild and temporary, others may require medical treatment. Maintaining good hygiene, eating a balanced diet, and seeking medical advice when symptoms appear can help prevent and manage many skin conditions.
